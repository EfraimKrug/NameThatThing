Cords - two power and two others

The power cords look (and may be) like your phone recharging cords - with the black box that plugs into the wall.

You need one for the Raspberry Pi, and one for the monitor. The monitor probably comes with one.

The first cord is called a USB to Micro USB... it is easier to deal with more cord then less, so I would suggest getting about 1 meter in length.

This cord will go from one of the USB ports on the Rasperry Pi to the micro USB port on the monitor. Is is going to be responsible for transmitting touch signals from the monitor to the Rasperry Pi.

The second is called an HDMI cable (both ends are the same). Again, it is easier to deal with more cord then less, so I would suggest getting about 1 meter in length.

This cord will transmit all the normal screen signals - everything will work with this cord alone (except the touch functionality).

Also notice, the ends on this cord are about as wide as my thumbs - i.e. wider than a USB.
